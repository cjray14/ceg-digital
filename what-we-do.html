Our vision

What we do

SEO-friendly h2 page heading

CEG Digital is proud to offer a suite of services to enable universities to expand global access to higher education programmes. Our vision is to become, in partnership with a select portfolio of high ranking universities, the leading global provider of online and blended higher education over the next five years.

How it works

Leveraging decades of global higher-education experience, CEG Digital provides a unique combination of financial resources, teaching expertise and global market knowledge to unlock the online learning market for both students and universities.

We can provide

  • Development of new and/or conversion of existing courses, in close collaboration with university academics
  • E-learning tutor recruitment, management and training
  • Locations for face-to-face workshops, as required
  • A learning management system
  • Dedicated pastoral and administrative support teams
  • Global sales team engaging with international agents, corporate and government channels
  • Direct sales conversion team, admissions support and enrolment services

As an example of our pedagogical expertise, watch this video to find out how students learn with Falmouth Flexible.

Why choose us

CEG Digital market university courses worldwide and, significantly, we provide all the up-front capital. Students are taught by online tutors who report to university faculty. The university retains full control and all IP of the academic material, and overall control of quality assurance.

Student support is key to the CEG Digital model - we know that online students need more support not less. Our course advisors provide a friendly ear for routine advice about work-life balance, how to be an effective online learner and the student’s progression through the programme.

We can also provide optional Face-to-Face Events for partner universities, designed to offer experiential learning by bringing students together with their peers and academic staff. Events are devised to allow exploration of topics related to students’ course content and offer a wider industry context for their learning.

Other benefits to universities include

  • Introduction of programmes to new, currently untapped audiences
  • Increased student volumes without stress on physical infrastructure: ability to scale rapidly
  • Foundation for a robust digital learning strategy
  • Development of newly structured programmes in areas of expertise
  • Access to a global marketing and sales network for online, blended and on-campus programmes
  • Opportunity to monetise historical and ongoing investments in open educational resources
